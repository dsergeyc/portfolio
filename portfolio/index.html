Case Study 01
Adobe.com
Checkout
Redesign
$36.6M ARR impact
Problem
Checkout was
fragmented.
  • Teams built similar flows independently — no shared UI
  • Inconsistent specs, high cost to maintain
  • Revenue-critical journeys slowing Growth experimentation
Before
The old checkout experience.
Step 1 — Email
Step 2 — Payment
Step 3 — Confirmation
Hypothesis
A guided, one-page checkout
will reduce drop-off
and increase conversion.

My role: Started as Design Lead, transitioned to Manager mid-program. Led a designer on this program while partnering with PM, Eng, PMM, and Growth.

Key Decisions
Three bets that
shaped the program.
Test first. Platform later.

Validate the concept before investing in full S2 component infrastructure. Let the experiment justify the platform.

Incrementally adopt the new design system.

System mid-migration from v1 to v2 — not all components were React-ready. Move forward with a plan to close gaps post-validation rather than wait.

Don't make teams wait for the platform.

Product teams kept shipping features while we built the reusable foundation in parallel — so no one was blocked waiting for infrastructure to be ready.

Case Study 02
Bundled
Merchandising
Platform
$14.4M ARR impact
Problem
A capability gap
with real revenue impact.
  • Customers couldn't select pre-configured bundled offers
  • Too many checkout steps — high drop-off
  • High engineering overhead slowing experimentation
Strategy

Unlock the capability at the platform level. Deliver incrementally while building the foundation.

The Trade-off
Speed vs. scalability.
I chose both — in sequence.
PM / Business
Ship fast.

Launch quickly. Minimal upfront platform investment.

VS
Engineering
Minimal scope.

Smallest possible version. Avoid future technical debt.

Leadership Decision
Run the experiment first — then fund the platform.

We aligned on validating demand before committing to full platform investment. The results made the business case. Outcome: validated and funded.
